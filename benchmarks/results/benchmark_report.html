Summary and Conclusions

Based on the benchmarks performed, the Rust implementation (networkx-rs) shows an average speedup of 9.03x over the Python implementation (NetworkX) across all tested operations. Out of 17 operations benchmarked, Rust was faster in 17 cases.

Key observations:

  • The performance advantage of Rust tends to increase with graph size, with a maximum speedup of 197.26x.
  • Graph creation operations show consistent speedup across all graph sizes.
  • The most significant performance differences are observed in operations that are computationally intensive or involve heavy memory operations.
  • For very small graphs, the performance difference is less pronounced, but still favors Rust in most cases.
